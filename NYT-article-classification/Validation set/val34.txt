AdvertisementBy Weiyi Cai, Annie Daniel, Jon Huang, Jasmine C. Lee and Alicia ParlapianoFeb. 13, 2021Now on the floorThe House managers are delivering their closing arguments on the Senate floor. The final vote is expected later this afternoon.If all 100 Senators are present, 67 votes are needed to convict.
	The Senate plans to vote Saturday on whether or not former President Donald J. Trump is guilty of inciting the Jan. 6 insurrection on the U.S. Capitol. At least 17 Republican senators would have to vote with Senate Democrats to reach the threshold for a conviction, two-thirds of senators present.

	Earlier Saturday, five Republicans and 50 Democrats voted to call witnesses, setting the stage for a prolonged trial. But after striking a deal with Mr. Trump’s defense team, House impeachment managers dropped their call for witnesses, clearing the way for a vote on conviction.

	In a separate vote on Tuesday, six Republicans joined Democrats to reject constitutional objections and allow Mr. Trump’s trial to proceed.

For the latest updates, follow our live coverage of the impeachment trial.

	For the votes on conviction, “guilty” votes are recorded as “Y” and “not guilty” votes are recorded as “N.”
